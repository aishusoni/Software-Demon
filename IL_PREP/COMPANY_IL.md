## 1. What They Do (Entity)

### Core Mission
> *"Digital-first solutions, grounded in research and guided by Curriculum-Informed AI™, purpose-built to support teaching and accelerate learning."*

They are a **K–12 ed-tech platform company** — not a tutoring marketplace, not a content aggregator. They build the actual curriculum software that schools and districts use as their primary instructional layer.

### Product Portfolio
| Category | Products | What it is |
|----------|----------|------------|
| Core Curriculum | Dragonfly, Imagine IM, StudySync, Traverse, Twig Science | Full-year courses in ELA, Math, Science |
| Supplemental & Intervention | Imagine Math, Imagine Language & Literacy, Imagine MyPath | Targeted support, adaptive/personalized |
| Courseware (6–12) | Imagine Edgenuity, EdgeEX | Online credit-bearing courses, credit recovery |
| Assessment | Imagine Assessment | Benchmark + formative assessments |
| Services | Certified teaching, tutoring, speech teletherapy | Human-staffed instructional services |
| AI Layer | Imagine AI / Curriculum-Informed AI™ | AI woven into the product — not a bolt-on |

### The AI Angle — Curriculum-Informed AI™
This is their differentiated positioning. IL's philosophy: AI must be grounded in **curriculum** (scope, sequence, pedagogy) not just raw LLMs. This is a technical and product differentiator — worth understanding deeply before your interview.

Questions to think about:
- How would you design an adaptive learning engine that respects curriculum structure?
- How is personalization at IL different from Netflix-style recommendations?

### Scale
- 18 million students
- 2 million teachers  
- 38,000+ U.S. schools
- ~50% of all U.S. districts

This is **enterprise B2B** sold to school districts (government procurement cycles, ESSA compliance, budget scrutiny). Not a consumer app.

## 2. How They Exist in the World (External Factors)

### Market
- **Segment:** K–12 digital curriculum and instructional technology (EdTech)
- **Customers:** U.S. school districts and state education agencies
- **Budget source:** Federal (ESSER funds, Title I) + state + district budgets — highly tied to **government funding cycles**
- **Sales motion:** Long B2B cycles, RFP-driven, relationship-based, compliance-heavy (ESSA effectiveness tiers matter)

### What Makes Them Stand Out
1. **Research-backed** — every product has efficacy studies (ESSA Tier 2 evidence). This is a procurement filter.
2. **Full portfolio** — from core curriculum to intervention to assessment to services. One vendor, K–12 coverage.
3. **Curriculum-Informed AI** — not plugging GPT in raw. They argue AI only works in education if it respects curriculum structure.
4. **Scale** — 50% U.S. district penetration is a moat. Switching costs are high in districts.
